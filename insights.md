\# Insights: Customer Retention Analysis



\## 1. Customer Base



The dataset contains registered customers with different purchase behaviors, including repeat customers, one-time customers, inactive customers, and customers with no completed purchases.



\## 2. Purchase Behavior



Repeat customers are important because they show stronger retention and higher business value. One-time customers should be targeted with follow-up campaigns to encourage another purchase.



\## 3. No-Purchase Customers



Some customers registered but did not complete any purchase. These customers may need onboarding support, discount campaigns, or clearer product recommendations.



\## 4. Customer Lifetime Value



Customer lifetime value identifies which customers contribute the most revenue. High-value customers should be prioritized for loyalty programs.



\## 5. Activity Status



Customer activity status helps classify customers as active, at risk, inactive, or no purchase. This makes it easier to prioritize retention actions.



\## 6. New vs Returning Customers



Monthly new vs returning customer analysis helps determine whether growth is coming from acquisition or retention.



\## 7. Churn Risk



Customers who have not purchased for more than 60 days may be at risk of churn. These customers should be targeted with win-back offers.



\## Final Recommendation



The business should focus on converting no-purchase customers, retaining repeat customers, and reactivating inactive customers through targeted campaigns.
